Chocolate Chip Cookies


Description

This is the best cookie recipe I've ever made. I've been asked by friends for years to make these whenever possible, and complimented for this recipe many times.

Every ingredient is important, and should be kept the same quantity. Baking is a science - experiment at your own risk! Having said that, the chocolate can be added onto or swapped out for any mix-ins. Pretzels, marshmallows, cranberries, and more are a great choice! Plan this recipe ahead, as it needs to be refrigerated overnight. The cookies should be taken out slightly before fully cooked - once the edges have begun browning, the cookies can be taken out and will cook fully outside the oven.


Ingredients

  • 0.5 cups granulated sugar
  • 0.75 cups brown sugar
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 0.5 cups unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1.25 cups all-purpose flour
  • 0.5 tsp baking soda
  • 220g chocolate, of your preference

Directions

  1. In a large bowl, mix the sugars, salt, and butter thoroughly.
  2. Whisk in the egg and vanilla, beating until completely mixed in.
  3. Sift in the dry ingredients, then fold with a spatula, making sure not to overmix the dough.
  4. Fold in the chocolate, or other mix-ins.
  5. Refrigerate the dough overnight, preferably, or for 30 minutes.
  6. Preheat the oven to 180 celcius, and scoop the dough onto a prepared pan.
  7. Bake for 12-15 minutes, and cool completely before serving.
  8. Serve and enjoy!
